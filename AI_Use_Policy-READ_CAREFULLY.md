# ⚠️ AI USE POLICY — READ CAREFULLY

This section is urgent and extremely important.

You are here to learn and grow. We don’t want you to waste your time — our priority is for you to get the most out of these projects and prepare you for the AP exam and higher education. Therefore, for these GitHub track coding assignments, **AI tools such as ChatGPT are strictly prohibited for writing or generating code**.

These assignments must be completed as though you are in an AP exam setting. The code you submit must reflect **your own thinking, logic, and implementation**.

---

## ✅ Allowed AI Use (Limited)

AI may be used **only** for help with Git and GitHub workflow questions.

### Examples

* “How do I stage and commit files?”
* “How do I undo my last commit?”
* “Why is my branch in a detached HEAD state?”

---

## ❌ Not Allowed

* Asking AI to **write, generate, or substantially modify your Java code**
* Asking AI to **solve the assignment or implement methods**
* Copying **AI-generated logic** and presenting it as your own

> ⚠️ If you are caught using AI to code these assignments, you will receive an **automatic 0**.

---

## 📚 Learning vs. Doing

AI *is* allowed when you are **learning the material itself** — reading, studying, or reviewing concepts. However, it should never replace your own thinking when it comes to writing code.

Try to avoid asking AI to explain everything from scratch. You don’t want to atrophy your brain — you want to build **strong mental models** that will actually stick for the AP exam and real-world programming.

---

## 💡 A Better Approach

1. First, form your own understanding of a concept in your own words.
2. Then, use AI to **cross-check or validate** your thinking.

### Example

Instead of asking:

> “ChatGPT, explain the difference between primitive types and reference types.”

Try:

> “I think of reference types like QR codes that point to data elsewhere, while primitives store the data directly. Is that an accurate analogy?”

And remember: you can always cross-check with me or any of the TAs — that’s what we’re here for.

---

## 📝 Final Notes

If you get stuck on one of the README explanation sections, don’t panic! You can still receive credit for **honest effort** — we want to see your thinking and process, not perfection.

However, effort does **not** excuse AI usage for coding.

If you’re ever unsure about what’s allowed, **ask first**. Reaching out is always the right move. We *love* to see students getting stuck and asking questions, because it means you’re being challenged and using that incredible brain of yours!
